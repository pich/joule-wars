# Joule Wars

**The next AI race will not be won by the smartest models. It will be won by the most efficient ones.**

Joule Wars is a concept coined by [Michał Piszczek](https://piszczek.pl/michal-piszczek): the
AI industry's shift from competing on **model capability** to competing on **energy efficiency**
— the contest to answer one question: *who produces the most useful intelligence per joule?*

> Canonical definition & FAQ: **https://piszczek.pl/joule-wars**

---

## Definition

**Joule Wars** is the transition of the AI industry from competing on model capability toward
competing on energy efficiency. As AI scales globally, electricity becomes the limiting
resource — so the decisive metric moves from *"how capable is the model?"* to *"how much useful
intelligence does it deliver per joule?"* It frames artificial intelligence, semiconductors,
cloud infrastructure, power generation and geopolitics as a **single economic system**.

```
WINNER = max( useful intelligence / joule )
```

## Intelligence per joule

The ratio of useful cognitive output (tokens, decisions, solved tasks) to the energy consumed
producing it. More fundamental than performance-per-watt: FLOPS per watt is dimensionally FLOPs
per joule (the seconds cancel). Power constrains the infrastructure; **energy is the currency of
the war.**

## Why it matters for embodied AI

Robotics makes the thesis stricter. A humanoid robot carries its entire energy budget on its
back (~1–3 kWh), and battery density improves only a few percent per year — there is no Moore's
law for batteries. Every joule spent on inference is a joule taken from the actuators. In
embodied AI, intelligence per joule stops being a cost metric and becomes a **design
constraint**. The precedent is mobile: Intel had the most capable chips and lost to ARM on
watts, because the device carried its own power.
